Over a year later...

February 21st, 2019

What a ride it has been, almost a year later.

After everything with interviews, I ended up working at Dematic in Waterloo. It was a neat place to work - I learned some interesting new skills related to instructional design with the course they had me working on for the entire second half of my 8-months there. I enjoyed working on my own project, the deep-dive into their help files to try and resolve the issues, a lot more - but it is what it is. It was good exposure to something new and different and something that I can take with me going forward - I now know how to properly design a course and an exam, to teach people how to do things! How about that.

I thought I might have ended up working as a technical writing co-op with Google (what I was referring to with that keeping open to relocation thing in my last post), but things unfortunately just didn't work out in terms of timing. It is what it is.

Now, the focus is on full-time jobs. Several resumes sent out with positive responses so far. I'm looking forward to seeing how it all works out in the end, once I've finished up my final school term. Crazy to believe that I did finally make it here, but I did it!

Time to keep pushing onwards and upwards.

Interview Time

February 2nd, 2018

Only three jobs that I applied to for this go-round using WaterlooWorks. A commute to Toronto did not sound appealing, so my choices in technical writing were somewhat limited as a result. But at the same time, I kept myself open to relocation! And man oh man am I excited about the opportunities that I have so far.

I've got two interviews coming up within the next couple of weeks. I'm really excited to see how they turn out, and just where I'll end up for my next workterm. It's going to be the start of 8 months of co-op work, so I want to have something good to look forward to.

In the meantime, I'm just trying to stay on top of schoolwork, and keep trying to add new things to this website. For now, that includes a proper title banner! And that feels like a pretty great accomplishment for right now.

Next, I think I'd like to add some more styling to this blog page, to make it a little more visually interesting. But that's something to consider later.

On another note, HackerNestKW also had the great privilege of putting together a successful Fishackathon just a couple weeks ago. Our winner will be going on to compete against the winners from the rest of the world-wide competition. It was a great feeling to pull the entire thing together, after being so anxious about it working out in the first place! But, in the end, we succeeded, and even managed do our usual tech social on the Monday after - talk about a long weekend.

Almost Done at OpenText

December 13th, 2017

It's been an interesting couple of months here at OpenText. It has been over four months at this point, but it honestly doesn't feel that way, for some reason.

The start of my job here definitely was not what I was hoping for: my manager being on vacation for my entire first week, and no one else who was actually in the office with me knowing what it was that I was supposed to be doing, exactly. My manager was out in the Ottawa office, which only made things more difficult. Compared to my last co-op, I can't exactly say that the onboarding was smooth!
